About Me

I am a researcher at the Center for Water and Climate Change Research (CRECC), affiliated with the Geological and Mining Research Institute (IRGM), under the Ministry of Scientific Research and Innovation of Cameroon (MINRESI). My research focuses on remote sensing, machine learning, and oceanography. I have gained extensive experience in MATLAB, Python, and R programming, as well as the use of geographic information systems such as Leaflet, PostgreSQL/PostGIS, QGIS, and ArcGIS. Additionally, I possess in-depth skills in machine learning, particularly in regression and classification using techniques such as neural networks, LSTM, SVM, RF, RL, DT, and KNN. Recently, my research has been primarily focused on coastal vulnerability issues in the Bonny Bay, along the coast of Cameroon.
